# Pepper Runs Off

Mia and Sam loved Saturdays. On Saturdays they went to the market with their puppy, Pepper. Pepper was small and brown, with floppy ears and a bright red collar.

The market was busy and loud. People talked and laughed. There were flowers, fish, and warm bread. Pepper sniffed everything. His little tail would not stop wagging.

Mia held the leash tight. "Stay close, Pepper," she said. Sam giggled. "He wants to see it all!"

Then a yellow butterfly flew past Pepper's nose. Pepper jumped. He pulled hard. The leash slipped right out of Mia's hand.

"Pepper, wait!" Mia called. But Pepper ran into the crowd. In one blink, he was gone.

Sam's eyes went wide. "Where did he go?" Mia took his hand. "Don't worry," she said. "We will find him. Let's look together."

First they ran to the flower cart. The flowers smelled sweet. A lady was selling red roses. But there was no little puppy here.

Next they checked the fish stand. A big gray cat sat on a box. The cat hissed. Sam jumped back. Still, no Pepper.

They went to the bakery last. The bread smelled warm and good. Sam's chin began to wobble. "What if we never find him?" he whispered.

Just then, they heard it. A happy bark! "Pepper!" they both shouted. They followed the sound around the corner.

A kind fruit seller was holding Pepper safe in her arms. She smiled. "Is this your puppy? He came to say hello. I saw his red collar and waited for you."

Mia and Sam hugged Pepper tight. Pepper licked their faces and wagged his tail. "Thank you!" they said to the kind lady.

They walked home together, hand in hand, with Pepper close by. That night, Pepper curled up between them and fell fast asleep. It had been the best Saturday of all.
