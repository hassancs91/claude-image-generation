# Narration Script: The Little Cloud

**Scenes:** 10 · **Total narration:** ~870 characters · **Dialogue:** light (2 spoken lines)
**Strategy:** single warm storyteller narrator (best for a short beginner story).
**Model:** `eleven_v3` preferred (audio tags below). If v3 is unavailable, fall back to `eleven_multilingual_v2` and use the **original** text (no tags) — expressiveness then comes from `stability` ≈ 0.45 / `style` ≈ 0.2.
**Voice:** _to be chosen — pending ElevenLabs MCP / voice_id._

Tags are kept to 1–2 per scene; `...` marks a gentle pause.

## Title clip (title.mp3)
- **original:** The Little Cloud
- **tagged:** [warmly] The Little Cloud.

## Scene 01 — mood: gentle, bright
- **original:** High in the sky lived a little cloud named Pip. He was the smallest cloud of all.
- **tagged:** [warmly] High in the sky lived a little cloud named Pip. [gently] He was the smallest cloud of all.
- **rationale:** Warm open, soften on "smallest" to set up tenderness.

## Scene 02 — mood: proud, loud
- **original:** Every day, the big clouds made rain for the fields below. Boom! Their thunder was loud and proud.
- **tagged:** Every day, the big clouds made rain for the fields below. [boldly] Boom! Their thunder was loud and proud.
- **rationale:** Bold punch on "Boom!" to contrast Pip's smallness.

## Scene 03 — mood: hopeful, trying
- **original:** Pip wanted to make rain too. But when he tried, only one tiny drop fell. Plip!
- **tagged:** [hopefully] Pip wanted to make rain too. But when he tried... [softly] only one tiny drop fell. Plip!
- **rationale:** Hope, then a small soft deflation on the single drop.

## Scene 04 — mood: hurt, small
- **original:** "You are too small," rumbled the big clouds. Pip felt smaller than ever.
- **tagged:** [sternly] "You are too small," rumbled the big clouds. [sadly] Pip felt smaller than ever.
- **rationale:** Stern (not scary) rebuke, then a sad fall.

## Scene 05 — mood: quiet, lonely
- **original:** So Pip drifted away, down low over a quiet green meadow.
- **tagged:** [gently] So Pip drifted away, down low over a quiet green meadow.
- **rationale:** Quiet, drifting calm for the location change.

## Scene 06 — mood: sad, wilting
- **original:** There, on the dry yellow grass, was one little flower. Its petals were drooping and sad.
- **tagged:** [softly] There, on the dry yellow grass, was one little flower. [sadly] Its petals were drooping and sad.
- **rationale:** Tender discovery, sad on the drooping flower.

## Scene 07 — mood: thoughtful, hopeful
- **original:** Pip looked at his one tiny drop. Maybe one drop was not so small after all.
- **tagged:** [thoughtfully] Pip looked at his one tiny drop. [hopefully] Maybe one drop was not so small after all.
- **rationale:** The turn — wondering, then a lift of hope.

## Scene 08 — mood: tender, giving
- **original:** He floated close and let his little drop fall. Plip! It landed right on the thirsty flower.
- **tagged:** [tenderly] He floated close and let his little drop fall. Plip! It landed right on the thirsty flower.
- **rationale:** Gentle, caring delivery for the gift.

## Scene 09 — mood: joyful, grateful
- **original:** The flower lifted its head and opened bright and pink. "Thank you, little cloud!" it sang.
- **tagged:** [cheerfully] The flower lifted its head and opened bright and pink. "Thank you, little cloud!" it sang.
- **rationale:** Bright, singing gratitude — the payoff.

## Scene 10 — mood: warm, joyful
- **original:** Pip smiled the biggest smile a little cloud can smile. He was just the right size to help.
- **tagged:** [warmly] Pip smiled the biggest smile a little cloud can smile. [happily] He was just the right size to help.
- **rationale:** Warm, happy resolution — "just the right size."
