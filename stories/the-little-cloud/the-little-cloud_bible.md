# Visual Bible: The Little Cloud

**Slug:** the-little-cloud · **Level:** beginner · **Style locked:** warm soft watercolor children's-book illustration, gentle rounded shapes, cozy lighting · **Aspect:** 4:5

---

## Style sheet (verbatim in every prompt)

> Warm, soft watercolor children's-book illustration. Gentle rounded shapes, visible soft paper texture, cozy diffuse lighting, a tender pastel palette. Hand-painted picture-book look, friendly and calm.

---

## Cast

### Pip — the little cloud (protagonist, recurring)
- **Identity:** a very small, soft, fluffy white cloud — the smallest cloud of all.
- **Face:** a tiny, simple, gentle face built only of two small round dark eyes and a small soft mouth. No nose, no eyebrows. Expression reads through eye-shape and mouth curve.
- **Body:** a single rounded puffy cloud body, pure soft white with the faintest cool-grey shadow underside. **No arms, no legs, no hat, no accessories.**
- **Scale cue:** noticeably smaller than every other cloud in frame.
- **Default expression:** hopeful and a little shy.
- **Distinguishing (repeat on HARD CUTs):** very small size; two tiny round eyes + one small mouth; a single un-split cloud body.
- **Anchored body-state per location:**
  - **In the high sky:** floating freely on the wind, drifting, nothing beneath it.
  - **Low over the meadow:** hovering gently just above the grass tops, casting one small soft round shadow on the ground — NOT resting on the ground like a solid object.
- **Consistent:** true

### The big clouds (recurring — scenes 2 & 4)
- **Identity:** a cluster of large, plump storm clouds, clearly much bigger than Pip.
- **Color:** white-to-soft-grey, heavier and darker underneath where rain forms.
- **Faces:** broad, proud, slightly grumpy faces (same simple eyes + mouth style as Pip, scaled up). Friendly-grumpy, never frightening.
- **Distinguishing:** big and rounded; darker grey bellies; a proud set to the mouth.
- **Anchored body-state:** always high in the sky, floating; never near the ground.
- **Consistent:** true

### The little flower (recurring — scenes 6, 8, 9, 10)
- **Identity:** one small single flower growing alone in a dry patch of the meadow.
- **Form:** a slim green stem with a few small leaves and one round blossom of layered petals.
- **Color (when healthy):** bright warm pink petals with a soft yellow center.
- **State varies — see Prop-state tracker.**
- **Distinguishing:** a single flower, alone on dry yellow grass; layered pink petals.
- **Consistent:** true (identity carried by ref; state carried by prose)

---

## Locations

### High sky (scenes 1–4)
- **Type:** wide open daytime sky.
- **Key features:** soft gradient blue sky, a few big fluffy clouds drifting, far below a glimpse of small green-and-gold patchwork fields. Lots of open air around Pip to show how small he is.
- **Mood:** bright, airy, a little lonely for Pip.

### Green meadow (scenes 5–10)
- **Type:** gentle low meadow seen from just above the grass.
- **Key features:** soft rolling green grass, one dry yellow-brown patch where the lone flower grows, a calm low horizon, warm sky above. Quiet and uncrowded.
- **Mood:** quiet, hopeful, tender; warms to joyful by the end.

## Prop — Pip's one tiny water drop
- A single small, round, glistening water droplet. Appears falling in scene 3, held/considered near Pip in scene 7, and falling onto the flower in scene 8. Exactly **one** drop — never a shower.
